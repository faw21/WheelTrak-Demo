Project Detail

More than 50%wheelchair users experience wheelchair breakdowns every 6months and one in three breakdowns leads to adverse physical, social, psychosocial and economic consequences to wheelchair users which increases the public health and personal burden. Majority of the breakdowns occur due to caster failures that cause a wheelchair to tip, user to fall out of the chair and get injured.Preventative wheelchair maintenance has been found to reduce the frequency of wheelchair breakdowns by ten-fold, but compliance with maintenance recommendations is extremely low because maintenance tools developed as a part of training programs are generic and not reflective of how and where the wheelchair is being used.

No tool currently exists to monitor wheelchair usage, predict caster failures and trigger maintenance reminders. To address this issue, we are developing a low-power, Internet of Things device that tracks wheelchair condition and leverages artificial intelligence tools to provide maintenance recommendations for casters and other critical wheelchair parts. This device will be a part of our ongoing development of IoT system called WheelTrak. It currently includes a smartphone application that connects with sensors mounted on the wheelchair (instrumentation to be replaced by IoT device) and cloud server for data storage and analysis. We plan to develop the cloud-based machine learning algorithms as a part of this IoT device development and validation

We will be recruiting wheelchair users to participate in our project. Prior to the start of the program, you will be informed of the study procedure and compensation arrangements. Your demographic data and maintenance and repair histories will be collected. A data logger sensor will be mounted on the rear wheel to measure use and wear factors. Data on wheelchair use parameters like shocks, vibrations, distance traveled, temperature and humidity) will be collected for a week.

The sensor will be recovered from you as a participant after completing data collection after a week. A study staff member will visit the participant for this purpose.

You and your caregiver will be contacted after 6 months to record the failures and repairs that occurred in that time.
