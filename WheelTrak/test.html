Overview

Globally, more than 250 million adults, aged 60 and older, experience moderate to severe disability that limits their mobility, activities of daily living, and participation in society. For the elderly population, wheelchairs are often needed for safe mobility and when they are appropriate and reliable, they contribute to well-being, independence and help to prevent or delay the need for long-term care. Unfortunately, data collected over the past two decades suggest that wheelchairs commonly fail, and this occurs most frequently (about ever 2-3 months) in the adverse environments common in low-middle income countries (LMIC) and rural areas of high-income settings. One-third of these breakdowns result in injury, depression, and hospitalization raising the public health and personal burden. Without a functional wheelchair, the user has to stay in bed and suffer from health complications such as pressure sores which may cause death.

WheelTrak enables preventative maintenance of wheelchairs and reduces frequent wheelchair breakdowns and corresponding health consequences experienced by wheelchairs users every year.
